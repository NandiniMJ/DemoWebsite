    • SIBLINGS LOVE

    If there are any siblings, it’s always a
    good idea to invite them to participate in the baby photoshoot.
    The poses will be highly dependant on the age and sizes
    of the kids.


    For example, if the older child is still not strong
    enough to hold a newborn, you can first
    position the newborn on the bed, a bean bag, etc.
    and just ask the sibling to lay on one of the sides.

    CANDID LOVE

    Any parent can tell you that babies are little angels when they
    sleep, but when they’re awake, things can get messy.

    Well, the same happens during a baby photoshoot!
    You’ll see all these beautiful, seemly effortless photos
    online, and you might think they sort of happen like that.

    Eye contact

    While babies are adorable in almost any pose,
    the technique of making eye contact is extremely effective
    for a great picture.

    If the baby is looking away, the viewer of the photo
    is kept as an outsider. Instead, if the baby is looking at
    the camera, you’ll make a connection that draws
    the viewer into the scene – it becomes much more intimate.

    Capture babies discovering
    the world

    This is one of the best photoshoot ideas for baby and
    parent bonding! There’s no rule saying that
    baby photoshoots need to happen in a studio or at home –
    you can always take them outdoors.
